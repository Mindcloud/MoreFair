You gain power based on the rankers above you, at your current ladder. The rate at which you gain power is also influenced by your bias and multi. Each bias increases the power gained, by one. Each multi multiplies that score (including the Bias) by its value.
You gain points based on your current power.

power += (rank + bias - 1) * multi;
points += power;

You gain multi and bias by sacrificing all your power and/or points. The cost is based on your current ladder.
For bias you need to sacrifice your points and for multi you reset with power, but also sacrifice all your points and bias.

upgradeCost = (currentLadder + 1) ^ (level);

You can promote to the next ladder, once there a few criteria fulfilled.
  • You need to be rank 1.
  • You need to have reached 100 million points * current ladder or more.
  • You need enough people at your ladder. The minimum amount to promote is 10, but it increases from 11 and onward by 1.
  • You need a big enough lead to your pursuer. This lead is generally reached after being ~30sec in front of them. (people can throw vinegar at you during this time)
  • You need to press the promote button, that will appear on the top of your screen.
Promotion leaves behind a body, that doesn't gain power or points, for the rest of the ladder to marvel at.
If you are at the last ladder with all your peers, the ability to promote turns into an Asshole-Button, which, if enough people press it:

RESETS THE GAME!

You can gain sour grapes in 2 ways.
  • Being last in a ladder that filled up, gains you 5 sour grape every second.
  • Getting passed by someone else, while having more than 1 multi, also rewards you with 1 sour grape.
Also, you gain vinegar based on your current grape-count. (if you are not #1)
This vinegar can be used to throw onto the top person, if they are rank 1. If they have more vinegar than you, you just subtract your amount of vinegar from theirs, but if you throw more than they can handle, you force them to buy a multi or reset their points to 0 if they can't afford it. The person on the top spot also looses 0.25% of their vinegar count every second.

There is a way that you can auto-promote, which unlocks from Ladder 1 onward. This feature, can be bought using your hard-earned grapes and completely bypasses the criteria, that you need a big enough lead to promote.
Also, it's completely server-sided, so once you have it, no amount of vinegar can hurt you this ladder, even if you are not online. The cost of this feature gets cheaper, depending on how far behind you are on your current climb.
Sadly, this feature is not available on the last ladder, which means no one, can escape the grapes of their competitors.

This guide is aimed at new players of MoreFairGame, who already read the rest of help section and clicked around in the game a bit, but are struggling to find a strategy to conquer the monstrosity that the very first Ladder of the game tends to be. This guide does not cover Grapes and Vinegar in detail, but to help out everyone stuck in Ladder 1 including yourself, it is a good idea to regularly throw Vinegar at afk players who stay at the top for a few minutes without promoting, especially so if their Power is high.

Leaving the first Ladder is far easier and faster right after the game resets for everyone. So you could just wait for that reset, but why not use the time to learn the mechanics and strategies of the game, and see how you can fare against the monster that Ladder 1 is right now until the next reset comes around.

The key takeaway if you do not want to read through the entire guide is to bring some patience, focus on buying Multis first, then focus on buying Bias to keep yourself at the bottom of the ranking and then finally just let the game run and hope you make it to first place and can hit the promote button.

Basic Strategy

The basic strategy boils down to four steps:

  1. Buy Multis
  2. Buy Bias
  3. Run for the Top
  4. Promote Button

Step 1: Buy Multis

In this step you buy Multis to improve your Power gain. Every Multi you get increases the multiplier for the Power you gain each second. That sounds great, so should we just keep buying more Multis whenever they appear? No, since each time you buy a Multi it completely resets your Power, Bias and Points, you should at some point commit to your current level of Multi and stop buying more. The decision to stick with your Multi is what ends this first step of the basic strategy. While building up your Multis you can also buy some Bias here and there, but it tends to not matter that much when there are many people in the Ladder.

Step 2: Buy Bias

In this step, you buy Bias. Intuitively, the effect of Bias is that each Bias lets you pretend to be one spot lower on the Ladder than you actually are. This is important because the game is balanced to give the players at the bottom of the Ladder more Power each second to help them catch up. However, a few points of Bias barely make a difference when there are hundreds of people in the Ladder. Does that mean you should neglect Bias? No, there is an easily overlooked second advantage that Bias can give you: Every time you buy a Bias, your Points will be reset to zero but your Power stays the same. This means, each time you buy a Bias, you are sent back to the very bottom to the leaderboard. That is a great thing, because it lets you accumulate Power faster than if you stayed at a higher rank. Therefore, in this step, you want to buy some Bias spaced out in a way to let you stay around the bottom parts of the Ladder. Power is very important in this game, because having higher Power than your competition is what will allow you to eventually surpass them in the ranking. However, ultimately Points are what makes you rise to the very top of the Ladder, so you should not keep resetting them by buying Multis or Bias forever. Instead, just like previously with Multis, this step finishes by committing to your current level of Bias.

Step 3: Run for the Top

At this point, you are done buying and all that is left to do for now is to watch yourself rise the Ladder and hope that you will make it to first place eventually.

Step 4: Promote Button

The final step is to click the promote button and go on to the next Ladder. Shortly after you hit the first place, a promote button appears at the top center of the page. You want to click this button before the other players push you out of first place again or, even worse, use their Vinegar to send you all the way back to the bottom of the Ladder.

The Basic Strategy gives you a solid framework that you can build your own strategies on. The next section will give you a detailed example strategy that you could try out. It is of course entirely possible to make the Basic Strategy work by just relying on your intuition instead of following a detailed plan.

Detailed Example Strategy

Step 1: Buy Multis

Start out by buying Multis whenever possible. Meanwhile, keep an eye on the levels of Power that the people at the first place tend to have. You stop buying Multis once the price for the next Multi would be close to or exceed half the average Power of first place you observed lately.

Step 2: Buy Bias

Start out by buying Bias whenever possible. Meanwhile, keep an eye on the number of Points that the people at the first place tend to have. You stop buying Bias once the price for the next Bias would be close to or exceed 20% of the current score of first place.

Step 3: Run for the Top

While aiming for the top you can compare your Power to the Power of those around you. If almost all the people around you have lower Power than you, everything is working well.If you still find yourself stuck in the ranking with insufficient Power to climb up, you could just buy another Bias and try again.

Step 4: Promote Button

Make absolutely sure to be around when you get close to the top. Just hit the promote button as soon as possible to give other players the least amount of time to mess with you. Being active right when promotion time hits is the only reliable way to ensure a safe promotion. Optionally, if you still fear being hit with Vinegar, you could chat a bit in the in-game chat while waiting to hit first place. Experienced players will likely see your name in chat and realize that using Vinegar on you would be a waste as you are going to promote anyways.

Note that this Example Strategy favors simplicity over perfect efficiency. Trying to time your Multi and Bias purchases optimally and deciding when it is the best time to stop buying can be very challenging! While the guide is mainly focused on the first Ladder, most of the concepts intruduced here are still very valid when tackling the rest of the game.

Where to go from here
